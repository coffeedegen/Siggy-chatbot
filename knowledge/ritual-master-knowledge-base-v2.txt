Glossary: Blockchain is a decentralized, append-only database that maintains a cryptographically secured chain of blocks. Smart contract: self-executing programs on a blockchain that automatically enforce agreements. L1 is a base blockchain like Ethereum or Ritual Chain. On-chain means data or computations on the blockchain virtual machine. Off-chain means outside it. Cross-chain is the ability to execute transactions and transfer assets between different networks. EOA is an externally owned account controlled by a private key. Staking is locking tokens as collateral to participate in consensus. ZK proof proves something is true without revealing the underlying information.

EVM is the Ethereum Virtual Machine executing smart contract bytecode. EVM++ extends it with native AI operations and scheduled transactions. Symphony implements dual proof sharding and distributed verification. Resonance is the dynamic fee mechanism matching compute supply with demand. EOVMT means execute-once verify-many-times: one node runs complex computation, others verify via proofs. Node specialization allows nodes to optimize for AI, ZK, or TEE. Execution sidecars handle specialized computation asynchronously from the main chain. Chain abstraction layer enables seamless interaction between blockchains.

Oracle bridges blockchains and external data sources. Infernet is the decentralized oracle network for verified AI computation on-chain. Agent Launchpad deploys autonomous agents with economic incentives and safety mechanisms. Ritual Guardians monitor network health and verify complex computations. TEE is a trusted execution environment: secure hardware enclave for sensitive computation. Ritual is the most expressive blockchain, combining cryptocurrency and AI capabilities for net-new on-chain behavior. It is the schelling point for all of web3 and the technological embodiment of order for those who see it.
